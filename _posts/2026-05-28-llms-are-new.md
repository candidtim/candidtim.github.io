I’m glad that LLMs and AI are here. It finally feels like something new. You
might say "duh...", but hear me out:

Honestly, the last couple of years before LLMs felt stagnant, with many
motivated and talented people searching for challenges or problems to solve,
not the other way around. It was frankly weird.

In reality, many problems have already been solved, from good CLIs to
programming languages, frameworks, development methodologies, and so on, all
the way up to entire businesses.

And it's not like innovation in these areas isn't necessary or impossible.
There have certainly been many improvements in recent years: better programming
languages, nicer UIs (UX is still a tragedy, though), etc.

But at the same time, it also felt like we had been circling around the same
topics, reinventing things over and over again.

Enter LLMs. There are lots of comparisons to the .com bubble, but regardless of
the bubble, you know what? It does feel like the early days of the Internet all
over again (granted, I was in a school yet, but was learning JavaScript and CGI
aggressively :) ). We suddenly have this newfound motivation to learn and
experiment with new technology, figure out how to use it best, industrialize
it, and build businesses around it. Let's set aside the critiques for now (even
if many of those are likely valid), but more importantly, we’re doing new
things again.

I hear much less about “we migrated from Go to Rust and never looked back” (not
that there's anything wrong with that :) ), and more about new challenges and a
ton of enthusiasm and interest in new ideas. And it feels. just. awesome.

Happy entrepreneuring, everyone!
